What am I looking at?

This tool scans the market with a frozen machine-learning model and ranks every company against its peers. Here's how to read what you see.

The five tabs

  • scan — the whole tradable market, ranked.
  • watch — names you're tracking, plus alerts when something changes.
  • book — your holdings and watchlist as one scorecard: value, P/L, model standing, risk flags.
  • paper — how the frozen model is holding up out-of-sample vs. its backtest.
  • markets — the top picks grouped by industry and theme, sized by market cap.

Click any name anywhere to open the company view — verdict, price, your position, model drivers, signals, AI read, grounded chat, and news. Words with a dashed underline explain themselves on hover.

BUY · HOLD · AVOID

The verdict is nothing more than the company's cross-sectional percentile — where its model score lands among every scored name today:

  • BUY top quintile — 80th percentile and above.
  • HOLD the middle — 40th up to just below the 80th.
  • AVOID bottom of the pack — below the 40th.

Model signal · percentile · decile

The model signal is a relative rank, not a predicted return or price target. Percentile runs 0–100 (higher = ranked above more peers); decile buckets that into 1–10. The drivers (SHAP) show exactly which fundamentals pushed the score up or down.

The AI read & ask

The AI read (after the drivers and signals on a company view) is a local language model's plain-English opinion, grounded to that company's filing (and, when available, recent news it explicitly attributes to a source). It's an interpretation of the data — not a price forecast. If the local model isn't running you'll see a grounded template instead; click Generate local-model read to run it.

ask is the same contract made conversational: question by question, it answers only from that company's computed data — every number in the reply is checked against the data, and when the answer isn't in there it refuses instead of guessing. No buy/sell advice, no price targets.
